How you'd use it.

Three concrete ways an AU or NZ property syndicator can deploy Homeshare on day one. None require new origination, brand changes, or commercial-term concessions.

What stays yours

Your existing wholesale syndicate

Asset origination, commercial terms, operator relationships, investor brand, fee schedule. No change to how you source or structure deals.

What Homeshare adds

A licensed retail wrapper

Licensed manager and trustee, deed-governed per-deal trust, retail onboarding, secondary market, tax-efficient pass-through, monthly distributions. Configurable per deal.

Net to you

A new addressable retail audience

Retail investors who today cannot access wholesale syndicates because of minimum-ticket thresholds. Investor-to-investor secondary exit, no fund-level redemption mechanism.

Three use cases

01
Retail-enable existing inventory

Take any wholesale syndicate currently restricted to accredited investors and make it retail-tradable in weeks. Your pipeline becomes the day-one inventory; no new origination required.

02
Buy out of the FMA queue

A new entrant faces 12–18 months of FMA work before issuing a single retail unit. At close, you step into an active engagement with a documented path to full FMC Act MIS authorisation in 9–12 months.

03
Brand and terms unchanged

The first retail deal launches under the acquirer's own brand. Fee schedule and unit count are configured per deal. The acquirer keeps full control over how each deal is priced and presented to investors.

What this is.

A licensed New Zealand financial product, ready to issue retail tickets. Pre-revenue, no live deal yet, ~A$2.3M invested to date. 100% founder-held, no external equity, no preference stack.

The licensed manager and trustee operate under an FMA-issued exemption that admitted the scheme to NZ’s regulatory sandbox in late 2025. A 9 to 12 month transition to full FMC Act MIS authorisation is in progress with the FMA and MinterEllisonRuddWatts (lead legal counsel).

The Master Trust Deed creates a per-deal trust structure. 36 clauses, drafted by MinterEllisonRuddWatts, executed 30 January 2026. Each acquisition becomes its own tax-efficient, ring-fenced pocket within the same scheme. Add a new deal in days, not months, without re-engaging the FMA or re-papering counterparties.

Why now.

Three reasons this window matters more than the deck does.

Regulatory window

The sandbox is open. First-mover positioning has a half-life.

Homeshare is the only NZ retail-fractional MIS to have executed under the current FMA sandbox cohort. The next entrant starts the licence application from zero, on a 12–18 month FMA timeline. That window does not stay open indefinitely.

Strategic precedent

Strategic acquirers buy distribution rails when the build math doesn't work.

Praemium acquired Iress's OneVue platform business in 2024 (A$1M cash + A$20M earn-out structured against funds-under-administration). Centuria acquired Augusta's NZ distribution platform in 2020. Pattern, not coincidence.

Founder transition

Available for a clean 60–90 day handover.

Founder-led for 24 months to date; ready to hand over to the acquirer's CEO, Compliance Lead, and independent directors. No legacy team to integrate, no pension overhang, no equity issues.

Three substantive workstreams remain on the path to full FMC Act MIS authorisation: PDS lodgement on the Disclose Register, external supervisor appointment, capital adequacy submission. All scoped with FMA and MERW.

In plain English
MIS
Managed Investment Scheme; the NZ regulated structure for pooled retail investment, governed by the FMC Act 2013.
FMA pathway
The FMA-issued regulatory pathway that lets Homeshare operate as a licensed retail MIS under a templated disclosure regime, with full FMC Act MIS authorisation completed in parallel.
Master Trust Deed
The constitutional document executed by MERW on 30 January 2026 that governs the scheme and every current and future per-deal trust.
Per-deal trust
A separate legal pocket within the scheme that holds one asset and has its own investors and unit register. Each is ring-fenced from the others.
Ring-fenced
Assets of one per-deal trust cannot be claimed against to satisfy liabilities of another. Cell-style segregation under Deed cl 2.2.
PIE election
Portfolio Investment Entity tax status; the NZ tax wrapper that caps retail investor tax at 28% via Prescribed Investor Rate attribution.
