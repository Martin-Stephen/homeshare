Section 01

Executive summary

Homeshare provides licensed retail-fractionalisation infrastructure for property syndicators in New Zealand. The transaction offers a strategic acquirer an FMA-regulated platform, executed legal framework, integrated counterparty stack, and a documented pathway to full FMC Act MIS authorisation, ahead of the AU Digital Assets Framework Act commencing 9 April 2027.1

Investment highlights

  1. FMA-licensed Managed Investment Scheme operating under a bespoke sandbox exemption. Homeshare Scheme Management Limited (FSP1008923) and Homeshare Trustees Limited operate under the Financial Markets Conduct (Homeshare) Exemption Notice 2025, in force from 1 December 2025 to 30 November 2027.2
  2. Master Trust Deed executed by MinterEllisonRuddWatts. The 36-clause deed (executed 30 January 2026) provides for ring-fenced sub-trusts (cl 2.2), per-sub-trust PIE election (cl 13.6, 25), an investor-to-investor secondary market within a ±25% AVM band (cl 12), and a smart-contract permission layer (cl 2.6).
  3. Seven material counterparty agreements executed or in place. Including the Property Management Agreement with Online Rentals Limited (trading as Ray White Hamilton), CoreLogic NZ AVM data licence, AML/CFT programme documentation with The Compliance Company, KYC vendor integration, and a 24-month engineering engagement with Miquido sp. z o.o. (Kraków).
  4. Retail platform built and deployed across production, staging, and development environments. The platform comprises retail onboarding (KYC/AML), tokenised unit register, Homeshare Wallet over a bare-trust Custodial Bank Account, primary and secondary market modules, NAV and distribution engines, and an automated PIE tax engine. No sub-trusts have been launched as at the date of this document.
  5. 9–12 month transition to full FMC Act MIS authorisation in progress. The transition pathway is scoped with FMA and MERW. An acquirer inherits an active regulatory engagement together with the prepared substantive groundwork (deed, entities, AML/CFT programme, Regulation 87 custodial-money assurance, appointed auditor).

Section 02

Business overview

Homeshare operates a retail investment platform that converts conventional property syndicate transactions into retail-tradable units. The syndicator provides the asset and commercial terms; Homeshare provides the licensed manager and trustee entities, the deed and sub-trust structure, retail onboarding and unit register, custodial banking, automated PIE tax attribution, monthly distributions, and an investor-to-investor secondary market.

Group structure

●Homeshare Group LimitedHolding company. 100% common, held by founder. Platform IP, domains and trademarks assigned.
├──Homeshare Scheme Management Limited (HSML)Licensed manager. FSP1008923. Sole director Martin van Blerk.
└──Homeshare Trustees LimitedLicensed in-house trustee under sandbox cl 6. Replaceable under Deed cl 19. Holds scheme assets per sub-trust.
└──Master Trust Deed (executed 30 January 2026, MERW)36 clauses. One deed governs the scheme and all current and future sub-trusts.
└──Home Trusts (sub-trusts)Each sub-trust holds one asset, has its own unitholder register, and independently elects PIE status. Ring-fenced per Deed cl 2.2. Architecture supports unlimited sub-trusts; the sandbox permits 10 Exempt Offers; first sub-trust to be selected and launched by acquirer post-close.

Regulatory position

The platform operates under the Financial Markets Conduct (Homeshare) Exemption Notice 2025, an FMA-issued exemption granting sandbox status from 1 December 2025 to 30 November 2027. The exemption provides relief from certain Part 3, Part 4 and Part 7 FMC Act requirements, replaced with a templated Offer Page per deal, a scheme-level Disclosure Statement, and a quarterly Investor Dashboard. Substantive work toward full FMC Act MIS authorisation has been undertaken in parallel.

Founder background

Martin van Blerk is the sole director and shareholder of Homeshare Group Limited and its operating subsidiaries.

In February 2022, Mr van Blerk co-founded Pixelmon, a digital collectibles project. The primary mint generated approximately US$70 million in sales. Following criticism of the initial public art reveal, Mr van Blerk delivered against the post-launch product roadmap and, in October 2022, sold a controlling interest in the project to LiquidX Ventures (Singapore). Animoca Brands subsequently participated in a US$8.0 million seed round in 2024. Mr van Blerk retains a minority founder interest in Pixelmon and is not involved in its operations.

This history was disclosed to the FMA during the sandbox admission process. The FMA admitted Homeshare to the sandbox following that disclosure. MinterEllisonRuddWatts has provided written confirmation of the FMA disclosure, available in the data room (Folder 01).
