A syndicator deal becomes retail-tradable units, then a monthly income and an exit.

Regulatory position

Homeshare is an FMA-licensed Managed Investment Scheme manager with an active regulatory engagement. The FMA admitted the scheme through a bespoke regulatory pathway (the Financial Markets Conduct (Homeshare) Exemption Notice 2025), which permits launch on a templated disclosure framework while the full FMC Act MIS authorisation is completed in parallel, at acquirer pace, with the substantive groundwork already done.

In plain English
MIS
Managed Investment Scheme, the NZ regulated structure for pooled retail investment, governed by the FMC Act 2013.
FMA pathway
The FMA's bespoke regulatory engagement that lets Homeshare operate as a licensed retail MIS under a templated disclosure regime, with full FMC Act MIS authorisation completed in parallel.
Master Trust Deed
The constitutional document executed by MERW on 30 January 2026 that governs the scheme and every current and future sub-trust.
Sub-trust
A separate legal pocket within the scheme that holds one asset (e.g. one property) and has its own investors and unit register. Each sub-trust is ring-fenced from the others.
Ring-fenced
Assets of one sub-trust cannot be claimed against to satisfy liabilities of another. Cell-style segregation under Deed cl 2.2.
PIE election
Portfolio Investment Entity tax status, the NZ tax wrapper that caps retail investor tax at 28% via Prescribed Investor Rate attribution.

Sandbox today, full MIS in 9–12 months, caps lift, scale unlocks.

Pathway

Every sandbox cap lifts on full FMC Act MIS authorisation. Three substantive workstreams remain, PDS lodgement, external supervisor appointment, capital adequacy submission, scoped with FMA and MERW.
