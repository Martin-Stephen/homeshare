The opportunity.

Three concrete ways an AU or NZ commercial property syndicator can deploy Homeshare. None require new origination, brand changes, or commercial-term concessions. Residential is the sandbox restriction; the deed defines Property as “real property”, so the architecture supports office, industrial, retail and multi-tenant commercial under full FMC Act MIS.

What stays yours

Your existing wholesale syndicate

Asset origination, commercial terms, operator relationships, investor brand, fee schedule. No change to how you source or structure deals.

What Homeshare adds

A licensed retail wrapper

Licensed manager and trustee, deed-governed per-deal trust, retail onboarding, secondary market, tax-efficient pass-through, monthly distributions. Configurable per deal.

Net to you

A new addressable retail audience

Retail investors who today cannot access wholesale syndicates because of minimum-ticket thresholds. Investor-to-investor secondary exit, no fund-level redemption mechanism.

Three use cases

Brand and terms unchanged

Every offer launches under your brand. Fee schedule and unit count are configured per deal. You keep full control over how each deal is priced and presented to investors.

What we built.

A licensed New Zealand financial product, ready to issue retail tickets on your next deal. Built for property syndicators across asset classes; the architecture is asset-agnostic and configurable per deal.

Compared with the alternatives
Build it yourself
24 to 36 months and A$3 to 4M before issuing a single retail unit. The FMA work alone is 12 to 18 months. You also need engineering, an AML/CFT programme, a registry, a custody arrangement and a secondary venue.
Use a generic retail broker
Sharesies, Hatch and InvestNow are built for listed securities, not single-asset property syndicates. No per-deal trust, no ring-fencing between deals, no PIE election, no AVM-anchored secondary band, no per-deal fee schedule control.
Partner with Homeshare
First retail offer in ~90 days, under your brand and fee schedule. Per-deal ring-fenced trust. PIE-elected. Configurable per asset. Built for syndicators, not generic retail.

The licensed manager and trustee operate under an FMA-issued exemption that admitted the scheme to NZ’s regulatory sandbox in late 2025. A 9 to 12 month transition to full FMC Act MIS authorisation is in progress with the FMA and MinterEllisonRuddWatts (lead legal counsel).

The Master Trust Deed creates a per-deal trust structure. 36 clauses, drafted by MinterEllisonRuddWatts, executed 30 January 2026. Each acquisition becomes its own tax-efficient, ring-fenced pocket within the same scheme. Add a new deal in days, not months, without re-engaging the FMA or re-papering counterparties.

Platform capabilities

The technology layer that makes the scheme operationally viable. All of it is deployed in production today.

Issuer console

List a new commercial property, configure the offer, publish the offer page, track subscriptions, run distributions, monitor compliance. The acquirer’s operations team manages the whole book from one screen.

Investor accounts

Self-service signup, KYC, custody wallet, holdings dashboard, tax certificates. Onboard once, invest in any current or future deal across the scheme.

Primary market

Applications, Establishment Target tracking, automated unit issuance, confirmations. Funds flow through the bare-trust custodial bank account; nothing settles until the target is met.

Secondary market

On-platform marketplace for unit transfers within a ±25% AVM band. Match-based settlement; no fund-level redemption pressure on the trust.

Distribution engine

Monthly net rental income, time-weighted attribution, automated credit to every unitholder’s wallet. No manual payment runs, no per-investor reconciliations.

PIE tax engine

Per-investor PIR attribution, imputation-credit attachment, withholding, IR860 reconciliation. Retail tax capped at 28% under the listed-PIE election; everything runs automatically.

Audit trail

Immutable log of every platform action: subscription, transfer, distribution, manager intervention. FMA-grade reporting and auditor reconciliation built in.

AML and KYC

Identity verification, PEP and sanctions screening, ongoing monitoring. Documented under the scheme-level AML/CFT programme run with The Compliance Company.
