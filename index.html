Executive summary

Homeshare provides licensed retail-fractionalisation infrastructure for property syndicators in New Zealand. The transaction offers a strategic acquirer an FMA-regulated platform, executed legal framework, integrated counterparty stack, and a documented pathway to full FMC Act MIS authorisation — ahead of the AU Digital Assets Framework Act commencing 9 April 2027.1

Investment highlights

  1. FMA-licensed Managed Investment Scheme operating under a bespoke sandbox exemption. Homeshare Scheme Management Limited (FSP1008923) and Homeshare Trustees Limited operate under the Financial Markets Conduct (Homeshare) Exemption Notice 2025, in force from 1 December 2025 to 30 November 2027.2
  2. Master Trust Deed executed by MinterEllisonRuddWatts. The 36-clause deed (executed 30 January 2026) provides for ring-fenced sub-trusts (cl 2.2), per-sub-trust PIE election (cl 13.6, 25), an investor-to-investor secondary market within a ±25% AVM band (cl 12), and a smart-contract permission layer (cl 2.6).
  3. Seven material counterparty agreements in place. Including the Property Management Agreement with Online Rentals Limited (trading as Ray White Hamilton), CoreLogic NZ AVM data licence, AML/CFT programme with The Compliance Company, KYC vendor integration, appointed auditor, and a 24-month engineering engagement with Miquido sp. z o.o. (Kraków).
  4. Retail platform built and deployed across production, staging, and development environments. The platform comprises retail onboarding (KYC/AML), tokenised unit register, Homeshare Wallet over a bare-trust Custodial Bank Account, primary and secondary market modules, NAV and distribution engines, and an automated PIE tax engine.
  5. 9–12 month transition to full FMC Act MIS authorisation in progress. Pathway scoped with FMA and MinterEllisonRuddWatts. An acquirer inherits an active regulatory engagement together with the substantive groundwork already complete: deed, entities, AML/CFT programme, Regulation 87 custodial-money assurance, appointed auditor.

Business overview

Homeshare operates a retail investment platform that converts conventional property syndicate transactions into retail-tradable units. The syndicator provides the asset and commercial terms; Homeshare provides the licensed manager and trustee entities, the deed and sub-trust structure, retail onboarding and unit register, custodial banking, automated PIE tax attribution, monthly distributions, and an investor-to-investor secondary market.

How the platform works

Source: Master Trust Deed; FMA Exemption Notice 2025. Solid arrows indicate the deal-to-units lifecycle; dashed arrows indicate funds flow.

Group structure

Sub-trusts are ring-fenced per Deed cl 2.2. Architecture supports an unlimited number; the sandbox permits 10 Exempt Offers; the first sub-trust is selected and launched by the acquirer post-close.

Sub-trust workflow

A new sub-trust runs from instantiation to live operations in weeks, not months — the deed, trustee, manager, and FMA posture do not change between deals.

Unit economics

At an indicative 71 basis points per annum on a single A$800,000 residential asset, the recurring Trust Service Fee is approximately A$5,700 per annum per sub-trust. Shared platform infrastructure (register, distribution engine, reporting) amortises across sub-trusts; incremental sub-trusts add revenue faster than variable cost.

Liquidity mechanics and key risks

The structural liquidity mechanics and key risks are summarised below. Risks are disclosed for diligence completeness; mitigants are included where the position is structural rather than residual.

Liquidity spectrum positioning

Homeshare’s structural position: one step up from locked-in property syndicates, materially below daily-NAV managed funds. Depth depends on investor base, sub-trust count, and the manager’s use of cl 12 levers (matching, warehousing, market-making).

Liquidity mechanics

MechanismEffectLimitation
Investor-to-investor secondary marketDeed cl 12; ±25% AVM bandOn-platform venue for matched buyer/seller transfers without sponsor wind-up.Does not guarantee a match. Depth scales with the investor base and the number of sub-trusts.
AVM-anchored fixed-price corridorCoreLogic, 4-monthlyConstrains transfers to within ±25% of independent valuation. Limits panic-floor outcomes.Does not assist in correlated-sell events; the floor is the floor.
Non-redeemable unitsDeed cl 12.11The scheme cannot suspend redemptions because no redemption right exists; structural protection against the “redemptions suspended” outcome.No fund-level exit; secondary market or eventual wind-up only.
Manager discretion under Secondary Market RulesDeed cl 12Permits manager to set matching logic, introduce transaction fees, warehouse stranded orders, or appoint a market-maker.Levers available to the acquirer at its discretion.

Founder background

Martin van Blerk is the sole director and shareholder of Homeshare Group Limited and its operating subsidiaries.

In February 2022, Mr van Blerk co-founded Pixelmon, a digital collectibles project. The primary mint generated approximately US$70 million in sales. Following criticism of the initial public art reveal, Mr van Blerk delivered against the post-launch product roadmap and, in October 2022, sold a controlling interest in the project to LiquidX Ventures (Singapore). Animoca Brands subsequently participated in a US$8.0 million seed round in 2024. Mr van Blerk retains a minority founder interest in Pixelmon and is not involved in its operations.

This history was disclosed to the FMA during the sandbox admission process. The FMA admitted Homeshare to the sandbox following that disclosure. MinterEllisonRuddWatts has provided written confirmation of the FMA disclosure, available in the data room (Folder 01).

Mr van Blerk is available to enter into a transition consulting engagement of 6–12 months following completion. Consideration for that engagement is to be agreed separately from the transaction consideration.

The transaction is structured as a targeted strategic sale with 3–5 qualified invitees; no public auction. Exclusivity is granted to a single preferred bidder at day 30. The preferred close is approximately 90 days from indicative bid, subject to FMA change-of-control consent.
